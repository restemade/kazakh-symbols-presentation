The Power of Ambiguity:
National Symbols, Nation-Building and Political Legitimacy in Kazakhstan

by Sabina & Nafissa Insebayeva (2022)

Presented by: Darina Zarypkhanova

Methods Summary

  • Main approach: analysis of historical events and political context shaping symbolic containers.
  • The article places strong emphasis on historical events.
  • Academic sources: scholarly articles, academic books, research on nation-building and symbolism.
  • Primary/material sources: banknote designs, national anthems (1992 & 2006), state iconography.

Ambiguity and Identity

  • National symbols in Kazakhstan are intentionally ambiguous.
  • Ambiguity balances competing identity narratives.
  • 1992 anthem: symbolic ambiguity to maintain unity.
  • 2006 anthem: more Kazakh-centered message.
  • Currency design reflects evolving identity politics.

1993 Banknote Series

Past-oriented, culturally Kazakh, yet carefully non-divisive.

Notably absent: Kenesary, Amangeldi Imanov, Isatay Datov, Alash leaders.

2006 Banknote Series

2011–2014 Series

  • Shift toward “Kazakhstani civic patriotism”.
  • New imagery: Samruk bird, doves, Mäñgilik El Arch, Ak Orda Palace.

Symbolic Strategy

  • Symbols create continuity with selected historical narratives.
  • Ambiguity unifies diverse groups without direct conflict.
  • Kazakhstan strategically blends ethnic, civic, and multicultural elements.

Strengths of the Article

  • Strong interpretive argument.
  • Rich historical and contextual narrative.
  • Addresses post-Soviet dynamics, ideological debates, northern separatism, Russia’s shifting role.

Limitations

  • Over-reliance on official and textual sources.
  • Methods not fully explicit.
  • No data on public reception.
  • No triangulation (surveys, visual coding, independent data).

Connection to Course Themes

  • Regionalism: diverse histories shape symbolic politics.
  • Shifts in public opinion: symbols adapted for ethnic & civic audiences.
  • Strategic & expressive behavior: symbols used for identity and political goals.
